# Risk, Issue, and Decision Management

This guide defines a structured approach to managing risks, issues, actions,
and decisions across clinical research, biostatistics, and analytics projects.

It provides a unified framework connecting:
- RAID log tracking
- Decision documentation
- Status reporting and escalation

---

## Overview

Effective project delivery requires:
- early identification of risks
- structured tracking of issues and actions
- clear documentation of decisions
- consistent communication through status reporting

These elements are integrated through three core artifacts:

- RAID Log
- Decision Log
- Status Report

---

## Definitions

### Risk
A potential future event that may negatively impact scope, timeline, quality,
or deliverables.

- Example: Delay in data availability impacting analysis timelines

---

### Issue
A current problem that requires resolution.

- Example: Missing or inconsistent data identified during analysis

---

### Action
A specific task assigned to resolve an issue or mitigate a risk.

- Example: Data management team to resolve missing data queries

---

### Decision
A formally agreed-upon outcome that impacts project execution.

- Example: Adjustment to analysis population due to protocol deviation

---

## RAID Log Usage

The RAID log serves as the central tracking mechanism.

Use it to:
- log risks before they materialize
- track active issues
- assign and monitor actions
- record decision summaries where appropriate

Reference:
`../templates/raid_log_template.md`

### Best Practices

- Assign an owner to every item
- Include due dates for actions and issues
- Update status regularly
- Flag items requiring escalation
- Maintain traceability to source (e.g., meeting, site visit, analysis)

---

## Decision Log Usage

The decision log captures formal decisions with supporting context.

Reference:
`../templates/decision_log_template.md`

### When to Use

Use the decision log for:
- scope changes
- timeline adjustments
- operational or process changes
- statistical or analytical decisions
- regulatory-impacting choices

### Best Practices

- Document rationale clearly
- record approver(s)
- link to impacted workstreams
- reference related RAID items where applicable

---

## Status Report Integration

The status report provides a structured summary of project progress.

Reference:
`../templates/status_report_template.md`

### Integration Points

- Risks and issues → summarized from RAID log
- Actions → tracked via RAID and reported as needed
- Decisions → included as “recent decisions” or “decisions needed”
- Milestones → tracked separately but reported here

---

## Workflow Integration

### 1. Identify and Log

- Risks, issues, and actions are logged in the RAID log
- Decisions are captured in the decision log

---

### 2. Assign and Track

- Each item is assigned an owner
- Due dates and status are maintained
- Updates occur during regular project reviews

---

### 3. Review and Govern

- RAID log reviewed in:
  - core team meetings
  - risk review sessions
- Decisions reviewed in governance forums

---

### 4. Report

- Key items are summarized in status reports
- Escalations are clearly identified
- Stakeholders receive structured updates

---

## Escalation Framework

Escalation should occur when:

- high-impact risks become likely or imminent
- issues remain unresolved beyond agreed timelines
- decisions are delayed and impacting delivery
- milestones are at risk

Escalated items should be:
- clearly flagged in the RAID log
- highlighted in the status report
- communicated to appropriate stakeholders

---

## Alignment with Clinical Operations

In clinical trial contexts:

- Risks may include:
  - enrollment delays
  - site performance variability
  - data quality concerns

- Issues may include:
  - protocol deviations
  - missing or inconsistent data
  - delayed site responses

- Actions often originate from:
  - monitoring visits
  - data review findings
  - cross-functional coordination

- Decisions may involve:
  - protocol interpretation
  - statistical analysis adjustments
  - operational process changes

---

## Summary

This framework ensures:

- structured tracking of risks, issues, and actions
- clear documentation of decisions
- consistent reporting and escalation
- traceability across the project lifecycle

Together, these elements support effective and controlled
project execution in regulated environments.
