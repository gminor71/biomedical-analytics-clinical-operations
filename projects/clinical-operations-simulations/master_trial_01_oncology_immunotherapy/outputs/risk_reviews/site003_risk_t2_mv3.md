# Risk Assessment

Site: Site 003  
Visit: Monitoring Visit 3 (MV3)  
Timeline Phase: t2 – Active Monitoring  

---

## Overall Risk Level

**Medium — approaching threshold for elevated operational risk**

---

## Risk Summary

Site 003 remains a high-enrolling and operationally engaged site. 
Source documentation is generally complete, and no critical compliance 
issues have been identified.

However, compared with previous monitoring visits, there is now clear 
evidence of persistent operational patterns affecting multiple areas 
of study execution.

These patterns are no longer isolated and indicate sustained pressure 
on site processes.

---

## Key Risk Drivers

### 1. Data Entry Timeliness

Delays in EDC data entry persist and have slightly increased, with 
entries observed approximately 5–7 days following subject visits.

**Risk Interpretation:**  
Delays appear workload-driven but are now worsening compared to prior visits.  
Continued persistence may impact data review timelines and overall data quality.

---

### 2. Query Management

Several queries originating from earlier monitoring visits remain open 
and now exceed expected resolution timelines (>21 days).

New queries continue to be generated at a rate similar to previous visits.

**Risk Interpretation:**  
The sustained imbalance between query generation and resolution indicates 
ongoing operational strain and increasing backlog risk.

---

### 3. Adverse Event Documentation

Adverse event documentation has improved in completeness and consistency.

**Risk Interpretation:**  
This represents a positive trend and reduces risk in this domain; however, 
continued monitoring is required to ensure sustained improvement.

---

### 4. Visit Window Compliance

An additional visit outside the protocol-defined window was identified, 
representing repeated occurrence across multiple monitoring visits.

**Risk Interpretation:**  
While individually low impact, recurrence suggests a developing pattern 
that may indicate scheduling challenges.

---

### 5. Treatment Administration Documentation

A repeated treatment delay and one dose modification requiring clarification 
were identified.

Documentation in the EDC does not fully align with source-level rationale.

**Risk Interpretation:**  
This represents an emerging area of concern with potential clinical relevance.  
Repeated occurrences increase the importance of ensuring accurate and 
complete documentation.

---

## Comparison to Baseline and Previous Visit

* **Baseline (t1):** Risk driven primarily by high enrollment volume  
* **MV1:** Initial operational impacts observed (data entry delays, documentation gaps)  
* **MV2:** Recurring patterns identified across multiple domains  
* **MV3:** Persistent trends now evident, with some worsening 
(data entry, query aging) and emerging clinical documentation considerations  

These observations indicate progression from early trends to sustained 
operational pressure.

---

## Comparison to Other Sites

Relative to other sites in the study:

* Site 003 continues to demonstrate the highest enrollment performance  
* Query volume remains elevated compared to lower-enrolling sites  
* Data entry timeliness is slower and shows signs of worsening  
* Minor protocol deviations occur more frequently  

While differences remain consistent with higher workload, the persistence 
of these patterns distinguishes Site 003 from other sites.

---

## Risk Trend

**Upward pressure — approaching escalation threshold**

Risk remains at a medium level; however, continued persistence or 
worsening of current trends may warrant escalation in subsequent visits.

---

## Recommended Monitoring Approach

* Continue routine monitoring frequency with increased focus  
* Prioritize:
  - resolution of aging queries  
  - improvement in data entry timeliness  
  - clarification of treatment-related documentation  
* Monitor for further recurrence or escalation of current patterns  
* Reassess risk level at next monitoring visit  

Escalation is not required at this time; however, the site is approaching 
a threshold where escalation may be considered if trends do not improve.
