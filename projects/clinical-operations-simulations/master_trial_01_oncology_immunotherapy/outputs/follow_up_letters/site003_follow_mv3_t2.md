# Follow-Up Letter

Site: Site 003  
Visit: Monitoring Visit 3 (MV3)  
Date: 4/07/26

---

Dear [Principal Investigator / Site Team],

Thank you for your time and continued collaboration during the recent 
monitoring visit. We appreciate the ongoing efforts of the study team in 
supporting subject enrollment and study conduct.

This letter summarizes key observations and follow-up items identified 
during the visit.

---

## Summary of Observations

Site 003 continues to demonstrate strong enrollment and remains a key 
contributing site within the study. Source documentation is generally complete 
and site engagement remains high.

However, compared with previous monitoring visits, several operational 
findings have persisted and, in some areas, increased:

* continued delays in EDC data entry following subject visits  
* presence of open queries exceeding expected resolution timelines (>21 days)  
* repeated minor deviations in visit scheduling  
* additional treatment-related documentation requiring clarification  

While improvements were noted in adverse event documentation, the persistence 
of the above items indicates ongoing operational pressure at the site.

---

## Required Follow-Up Actions

Please address the following items:

### 1. Query Resolution

Several queries originating from prior monitoring visits remain open and 
have exceeded expected resolution timelines.

* Please prioritize review and resolution of all outstanding queries.  
* Ensure timely responses to newly issued queries to prevent further backlog.

---

### 2. Data Entry Timeliness

Data entry delays of approximately 5–7 days following subject visits were observed.

* Please ensure that study data are entered into EDC as soon as possible 
following each visit.  
* Continued delays may impact data review timelines and overall study efficiency.

---

### 3. Treatment Documentation

Repeated treatment delay and dose modification documentation requiring 
clarification were identified.

* Please ensure that all treatment-related decisions are clearly documented and
aligned between source records and EDC.  
* Complete and accurate documentation is essential to support clinical 
interpretation.

---

### 4. Visit Window Compliance

Additional instances of visits occurring outside protocol-defined 
windows were observed.

* Reinforce scheduling practices to ensure adherence to protocol visit windows.  
* Continued recurrence may require further review.

---

## Closing

At this time, findings remain manageable; however, continued persistence of
these trends may impact overall study conduct if not addressed.

Timely resolution of the above items will be important to support ongoing 
data quality and compliance.

Please do not hesitate to reach out with any questions or if additional 
clarification is needed.

Thank you again for your continued collaboration.
