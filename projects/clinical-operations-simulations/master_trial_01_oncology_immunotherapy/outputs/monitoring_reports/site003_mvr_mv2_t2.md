# Monitoring Visit Report

Site: Site 003
Visit: Monitoring Visit 2 (MV2)
Timeline Phase: t2 – Active Monitoring

---

## Visit Information

**Visit Type:** Routine Monitoring Visit
**Site:** Site 003
**CRA:** GAM
**Visit Date:** 3/24/26

---

## Site Status Summary

Site 003 continues to demonstrate strong enrollment performance and remains the 
highest enrolling site in the study. Site personnel remain engaged and 
responsive, and source documentation is generally well maintained.

As subject volume has increased, operational workload at the site has 
also increased. This is beginning to impact certain aspects of study execution, 
particularly data entry timeliness and query resolution.

---

## Enrollment Review

Site 003 has continued to enroll subjects at a steady pace, with a current
total of 16 subjects associated with site activity.

Subjects are distributed across multiple treatment cycles, with several 
subjects progressing through later cycles of therapy. 
Enrollment remains appropriate and consistent with site capability.

---

## Follow-Up from Previous Monitoring Visit

Follow-up actions from the prior monitoring visit were reviewed.

* Missing laboratory unit entries identified during the previous visit have 
been addressed.
* Improvements were observed in adverse event narrative documentation, 
though consistency remains variable.
* Data entry timeliness remains an ongoing issue, with continued delays 
observed for several recent visits.

Several queries from the previous monitoring cycle remain open beyond the 
expected resolution timeframe, indicating a need for improved query management.

---

## Data Review

### Query Management

The current query volume remains manageable; however, a subset of queries 
from the previous monitoring cycle remains unresolved and has exceeded 
expected turnaround timelines.

New queries generated during this review are consistent with prior 
findings and primarily relate to:

* adverse event documentation clarification
* visit timing confirmation
* data entry completeness
* treatment administration documentation

The presence of both new and aging queries suggests a developing pattern 
in query resolution timeliness rather than isolated issues.

---

### Data Entry Timeliness

Source documentation reviewed during the visit was generally complete and 
available for review.

However, data entry into the EDC system continues to occur several days after 
visit completion, with delays of approximately 4–6 days observed for recent 
visits. 
This represents a continuation of the pattern identified during the 
previous monitoring visit.

The delay appears related to operational workload rather than absence of 
source documentation.

---

### Visit Window Compliance

Most visits continue to fall within protocol-defined windows.

An additional visit occurring outside the protocol window was identified 
during this review. While the deviation remains minor and attributable to 
scheduling constraints, the recurrence of this issue warrants continued 
monitoring.

---

## Safety Review

Adverse event reporting remains appropriate, with reported events consistent 
with expected treatment-related toxicities in this study population.

Events reported include:

* fatigue
* nausea
* mild infusion-related reactions

All events remain within expected severity ranges, and no serious adverse events
have been reported at this site.

One subject experienced a treatment delay associated with laboratory review. 
While the delay was documented, the rationale recorded in the EDC requires 
clarification to ensure consistency with source documentation and protocol 
expectations.

---

## Findings

Findings identified during this visit are primarily consistent with those 
observed during the previous monitoring visit, with evidence of both partial 
improvement and ongoing recurrence.

Key findings include:

1. Continued delays in EDC data entry following subject visits
2. Recurring need for clarification in adverse event narrative documentation
3. Presence of aging open queries exceeding expected resolution timelines
4. Repeated minor visit window deviation
5. Incomplete documentation of treatment delay rationale

These findings are considered moderate in operational impact and reflect 
increasing workload rather than fundamental compliance deficiencies.

---

## Follow-Up Required

The following follow-up actions are required:

1. Resolve all open queries from prior monitoring cycles within expected timelines.
2. Ensure adverse event narratives consistently include complete and clinically 
relevant detail.
3. Improve timeliness of data entry following subject visits.
4. Reinforce adherence to protocol-defined visit windows.
5. Clarify and document treatment delay rationale in alignment with source 
documentation.

These items have been communicated to site staff and will be reviewed at 
the next monitoring visit.

---

## Monitoring Assessment

Site 003 remains an operationally strong and productive site. However, 
increasing subject volume is beginning to introduce measurable operational 
pressure.

Compared with the previous monitoring visit, there is evidence of:

* partial improvement in documentation quality
* persistence of data entry delays
* emergence of query aging
* recurrence of minor scheduling deviations

Overall, the site is demonstrating early signs of trend development in 
operational performance metrics. These trends do not currently represent 
significant compliance risk but warrant continued close monitoring.

---

## Risk Level

**Medium — volume-driven with emerging operational trends**

Risk remains associated with enrollment volume and increasing workload. 
At this stage, no escalation is required; however, continued observation is 
necessary to determine whether current patterns stabilize or progress.
