# Monitoring Visit Scenario — MV3  
**Site:** Site 003 (High Enrolling Site)  
**Timeline Phase:** t2_active_monitoring

---

## Visit Overview

This visit represents the third routine monitoring visit for Site 003 during active monitoring.

The site continues to enroll subjects at a high rate and remains a key contributor to overall study recruitment. However, increasing subject volume continues to place pressure on site operations.

This visit focuses on:

- assessing resolution of prior findings  
- evaluating persistence of operational trends  
- determining whether current patterns require escalation  

---

## Enrollment Snapshot

Total subjects associated with site activity: **18**

Enrollment remains strong, with several subjects progressing into later treatment cycles.

---

## Follow-Up from Previous Visits

### Improvements
- Several previously open queries have been resolved.
- Adverse event documentation shows improved completeness and consistency.

### Ongoing Issues
- Data entry delays continue to be observed.
- A subset of queries remains open beyond expected timelines.

---

## Data Review Observations

### Query Environment

The total number of open queries remains elevated.

Although some queries from previous visits have been resolved, new queries continue to be generated at a similar rate.

Several queries originating from earlier monitoring visits remain open, now exceeding expected resolution timelines.

This indicates a continued imbalance between query generation and resolution.

---

### Data Entry Timeliness

Delays in EDC data entry persist, with some recent visits entered **5–7 days after completion**.

This represents a slight worsening compared to the previous monitoring visit.

The delay continues to appear related to workload and competing priorities.

---

### Visit Window Compliance

An additional visit outside the protocol-defined window was identified.

While still minor in isolation, this represents a repeated pattern across multiple monitoring visits.

---

## Safety and Treatment Review

Adverse event reporting has improved in completeness and clarity.

However, treatment administration review identified:

- a second instance of treatment delay  
- one instance of dose modification requiring clarification  

In both cases, documentation in the EDC does not fully align with source-level rationale and requires clarification.

---

## Operational Assessment

Positive indicators:
- strong enrollment continues  
- improved AE documentation  
- continued site engagement  

Areas of concern:
- persistent data entry delays  
- ongoing query backlog  
- repeated visit window deviations  
- emerging treatment documentation inconsistencies  

---

## Initial Monitoring Impression

Site 003 remains a high-performing site from an enrollment perspective.

However, compared with previous visits, there is now clearer evidence of sustained operational pressure affecting multiple areas of study conduct.

While no critical compliance issues have been identified, the persistence of these trends suggests increasing risk if not addressed.
