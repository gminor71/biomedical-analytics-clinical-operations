# Trial Overview — Master Trial 01

## Study Summary

Master Trial 01 is a simulated Phase II oncology immunotherapy trial designed to support clinical operations training through scenario-based learning.

The trial environment is operationally realistic but intentionally simplified to support focused skill development.

---

## Key Characteristics

* Multicenter study with multiple site profiles
* Open-label, single-arm design
* Immunotherapy administered every 3 weeks
* Approximate enrollment target: 120 subjects

---

## Simulation Objectives

This master trial supports training in:

* Site monitoring workflows
* Risk-based monitoring concepts
* Query and deviation management
* Safety oversight and escalation
* Operational decision-making across time

---

## Site Structure (Simulation Roles)

* **Site 001:** Model site (low risk)
* **Site 002:** Typical site (minor variability)
* **Site 003:** High enrolling site (primary CRA focus)
* **Site 004:** Safety-focused site (higher operational risk)

Sites intentionally demonstrate normal variation rather than constant issues.

---

## Timeline Approach

The trial evolves through scenario phases:

* Startup context (reference)
* Early enrollment
* Active monitoring
* Risk escalation
* Pre-database lock

Each phase introduces operational changes rather than independent cases.

---

## Data Philosophy

Datasets are:

* Operationally realistic
* Simplified for training purposes
* Structured to support future analytical exercises

Data domains include:

* Subjects
* Visits
* Labs
* Adverse Events
* Dose administration
* Imaging
* Queries

---

## Role Perspective

Simulation work is performed from the perspective of a CRA assigned primarily to Site 003 while maintaining awareness of trial-wide trends.

---

## Relationship to Program

This trial sits within the broader biomedical-analytics-clinical-operations program and connects:

Clinical Operations → Data Quality → Statistical Analysis → Analytics
